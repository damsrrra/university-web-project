Marine Protection

Introduction

Marine protection, also known as ocean conservation, is the protection and preservation of ecosystems in oceans and seas through planned management in order to prevent the over-exploitation of these marine resources.

The targets affects to Marine protection

Everyone can help to make sure that we meet the Global Goals. Use these ten targets to create action to conserve and sustainably use the oceans.

Target 14.1 - REDUCE MARINE POLLUTION
By 2025, prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds, particularly from land-based activities, including marine debris and nutrient pollution.

Target 14.2 - PROTECT AND RESTORE ECOSYSTEMS
By 2020, sustainably manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems to avoid significant adverse impacts, including by strengthening their resilience and taking action for their restoration.

Target 14.3 - REDUCE OCEAN ACIDIFICATION
Minimize and address the impacts of ocean acidification, including through enhanced scientific cooperation at all levels.

Target 14.4 - SUSTAINABLE FISHING
By 2020, effectively regulate harvesting and end overfishing, illegal, unreported, and unregulated fishing to restore fish stocks.

Target 14.5 - CONSERVE COASTAL AND MARINE AREAS
By 2020, conserve at least 10% of coastal and marine areas, consistent with national and international law.

Target 14.6 - END SUBSIDIES CONTRIBUTING TO OVERFISHING
By 2020, prohibit subsidies contributing to overfishing and ensure fair treatment for developing countries.

Why marine protection is Important

Significant Portion of the World's Oxygen

Oceans and seas produce about 50% of the world’s oxygen through phytoplankton. Protecting marine life ensures a stable oxygen supply.

Regulating the Climate

Oceans absorb and store heat, regulating global temperatures and weather patterns. Their circulation systems prevent extreme climate shifts.

Supporting Diverse Marine Ecosystems

Marine ecosystems support biodiversity, offering habitats to countless species. Conservation helps maintain ecological balance.

Actions to Protect Marine Life

  • Reduce Marine Pollution: Avoid plastic waste, properly dispose of chemicals.
  • Protect Marine Ecosystems: Preserve coral reefs, support conservation programs.
  • Promote Sustainable Fishing: Avoid overfishing, follow responsible seafood practices.
  • Combat Ocean Acidification: Reduce CO₂ emissions to protect marine biodiversity.
  • Support Marine Conservation Policies: Advocate for stronger environmental regulations.
  • Increase Awareness: Educate communities and participate in ocean clean-ups.
